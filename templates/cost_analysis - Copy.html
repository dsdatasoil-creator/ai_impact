Workforce Optimization ROI

Measures the return on investment achieved by using AI to maintain the same level of output with fewer employees or fewer labor hours. The value comes from labor cost savings while preserving business performance.

Example

A company employs 100 people to process 10,000 customer requests per month. After implementing AI, employee productivity increases by 25%. The company can now process the same 10,000 requests with only 80 employees. The labor cost savings generated by reducing workforce requirements are compared against the AI investment cost to calculate the ROI.

Active Mode

Productivity Growth ROI

Measures the return on investment achieved by using AI to increase output while maintaining the same workforce size. Instead of reducing headcount, employees use the time saved by AI to perform additional work, serve more customers, or generate additional revenue.

Example

A company employs 100 people to process 10,000 customer requests per month. After implementing AI, productivity increases by 25%, but the company keeps all 100 employees. The workforce can now process 12,500 requests per month. The additional value or revenue generated by the extra 2,500 requests is compared against the AI investment cost to calculate the ROI.
